Do your parents smoke? Do you wish they didn't? Parents know that smoking is bad for them, for their wallet, and for you. The problem is that smoking is addictive and very hard to quit. There are many different ways to quit smoking, and many of them require the loving support of families. Often, parents also need some nudging and encouragement to get started. We provide you with an easy way of giving them the encouragement and the tools they need.

Our app and website allows you to track your parent's smoking habits, send reports to update them on their progress, text message them with encouraging motivational messages, and provide resources for you and for them to seek out to help them quit. MomQuitSmoking! and YOU will help them kick the habit!

FAQ

Why should I help my parents quit smoking?
There are many reasons for your parents to quit smoking. For them, it is a major cause of cancer and heart disease. It is also very expensive. For you, the dangers of second-hand smoke are also well known. Not only are you more likely to develop those problems, it is known that children of smokers are more than twice as likely to eventually take up smoking themselves. Any help you can provide your parents that moves them toward quitting smoking will benefit both of you greatly.
Will my parents be upset at me?
Talk to them. The best thing you can do is try to have an open, honest discussion with them about why you want them to stop and what benefits they (and you) will get out of quitting. While it is true that some parents may react negatively, it is important for you to be supportive and help them.
